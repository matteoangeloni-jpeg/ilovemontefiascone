San Lorenzo Nuovo: A Planned Hill Town near Lake Bolsena

San Lorenzo Nuovo stands on the northern side of Lake Bolsena in Lazio. Its regular eighteenth-century town plan gives it a different character from the older medieval settlements around the lake.

Why visit San Lorenzo Nuovo?

The town is associated with an eighteenth-century refoundation and a regular urban layout. That planning history makes it a useful contrast to the medieval fabric found in other Tuscia villages.

Include it in a northern-lake route

From Montefiascone, San Lorenzo Nuovo can form part of a broader route with Grotte di Castro, Gradoli and other Lake Bolsena villages.

What to verify before setting out

Opening arrangements and visitor services can vary. If a specific interior, museum or service is essential, verify current information through official local sources.

Plan from Montefiascone

Montefiascone provides a useful base on the opposite side of the lake, combining viewpoints, old-town walks and connections with the wider Tuscia area.
