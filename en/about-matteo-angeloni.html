Author profile

Matteo Angeloni

Author and editorial curator of I Love Montefiascone, with a background that connects academic research, public communication, public affairs and place-based storytelling.

Profile

Matteo Angeloni is a PhD candidate at the University of Tuscia and the editorial curator of I Love Montefiascone. His work focuses on the relationship between communication, technological innovation, public policy and territorial value.

Alongside his academic research, he develops editorial and communication projects with a specific focus on Tuscia, digital languages and the impact of artificial intelligence on economic and social processes.

Current academic position

Since 2024, he has been a PhD candidate in Societies in Transformation: Politics, Rights and Security (40th cycle) at the Department of Economics, Engineering, Society and Enterprise (DEIM), University of Tuscia, Viterbo, Italy.

Education

  • 2023-2024: Second-level Master's degree in Interest Representation: Lobbying and Advocacy, Sapienza University of Rome. Thesis: The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Lobbying: Strategies and Future Prospects in Policy Dialogue. Grade: 110/110 cum laude.
  • 2019-2021: Master's degree in Public, Digital and Corporate Communication, University of Perugia. Experimental thesis on augmented reality and user-generated content. Grade: 110/110 cum laude.
  • 2015-2018: Bachelor's degree in Communication Sciences, University of Bologna. Experimental thesis on the tourism revitalisation of Civita di Bagnoregio. Grade: 87/110.

Professional experience

  • 2023-2024: Public Affairs Support at Samsung Electronics Italia, Milan. Work included stakeholder mapping, briefing materials, external relations support and corporate positioning initiatives.
  • 2021: Social Media Manager at Lunaway, Viterbo. Managed social strategy and content production in support of digital communication, audience engagement and brand positioning.

Publications

Book: M. Angeloni, Il fenomeno Civita. Le azioni di marketing dietro un successo internazionale, Intermedia Edizioni, 2020, ISBN 978-88-31410-01-4, 90 pages.

Conference presentations

  • 21 May 2026: speaker at the international workshop The EU's Path Towards Digital Sovereignty & Strategic Autonomy at UNED in Madrid, with the paper Generative AI and the European Digital Single Market: Market Concentration and SME Competitiveness.
  • 4 October 2025: speaker at the 6th annual ICON-S Italia Conference at the University of Cagliari, in the panel Il lato buono dell'IA: l'erogazione dei servizi pubblici, with a presentation on artificial intelligence in education and training.
  • 26 September 2025: speaker at La Ricerca in Pillole, within the European Researchers' Night at the University of Tuscia, with a talk on artificial intelligence and reskilling.
  • 1-4 August 2025: poster presentation at the IRIS-AI Symposium in Montefiascone titled The Reskilling Paradox in the Age of Artificial Intelligence: Between Automation and Human Enhancement.
  • 16 June 2025: speaker at the 7th Ventotene Summer School and Conference Europa e Nuove Tecnologie, in the panel on artificial intelligence and fundamental rights, with a presentation on generative AI in EdTech.
  • 14 April 2025: speaker at L'economia di ChatGPT. Tra false paure e rischi reali, part of the seminar cycle Diritto e Comunicazione at the University of Tuscia.
