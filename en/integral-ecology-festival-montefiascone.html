The Integral Ecology Festival in Montefiascone

Every summer, between the old town and Rocca dei Papi, Montefiascone hosts the Integral Ecology Festival: four days of talks, reflection and shared culture aimed at visitors looking for something different from the usual music or food events. The 2026 edition, the sixth, is titled "Beyond the Deserts" and runs from 2 to 5 July.

This isn't a festival just for specialists: the talks are public and open to anyone visiting Montefiascone or Lake Bolsena in those days, and the opening on 2 July, with Mogol as guest, is designed for a broad audience.

Why it's worth going

The Integral Ecology Festival isn't only about the environment. The programme weaves sustainability together with territory, community and a more spiritual, cultural dimension, in a format that has no real equivalent elsewhere on Montefiascone's summer calendar. It's a chance to see the town from a less obvious angle: not just the Rocca, wine tastings and walks, but also a public space for discussion on issues that shape the future of the area and the people who live there.

For visitors staying by Lake Bolsena, it's also a way to break up a beach-and-food holiday with something different: an afternoon or evening built around a shared cultural theme, in an intimate setting like Piazza Santa Lucia Filippini.

The 2026 theme: Beyond the Deserts

The 2026 title, "Beyond the Deserts", evokes the idea of moving through moments of social or environmental dryness towards something more fertile: community, care for the land, mutual listening. It's more of an image than a technical programme, and it works as a thread tying the festival's different talks together without reducing them to a single topic.

For visitors who don't know the festival, this is mostly useful as an orientation: the talks touch on ecology, culture and community life, but they start from an idea of crossing through and shared renewal, not from a narrow environmentalist agenda.

The opening with Mogol

The 2026 edition opens on Thursday 2 July at 18:00 in Piazza Santa Lucia Filippini, in Montefiascone's old town. The opening guest is Mogol.

The rest of the programme for the following days, including any further guests and the detailed schedule of talks, is not yet fully public at the time of this guide: for operational details, the official channels of the festival and of the Municipality of Montefiascone remain the reference.

The festival is organised by Associazione Rocca dei Papi per un'Ecologia Integrale ETS.

Who it suits

  • Visitors to Montefiascone with a cultural interest, not just a scenic or food-focused one.
  • Families and groups interested in a public evening event in a relaxed setting.
  • Visitors staying by Lake Bolsena who want an alternative to the usual music events or food fairs.
  • Visitors in Montefiascone in summer who want to pair the beach-and-wine programme with something more reflective.

What to pair with your visit

To build a fuller visit around the festival, pair it with Rocca dei Papi and a walk through the old town, a day by Lake Bolsena, the rest of the Summer 2026 calendar and, for wine lovers, a stop dedicated to Est! Est!! Est!!!. For practical planning, the pages on where to stay and how to get to Montefiascone are useful too.

Before you travel

Frequently asked questions

When is the Integral Ecology Festival 2026 held?

The Integral Ecology Festival 2026 runs from 2 to 5 July, with an official opening on 2 July at 18:00.

Where is the Festival opening held?

The opening takes place in Piazza Santa Lucia Filippini, in the old town of Montefiascone.

Who opens the 2026 edition?

The opening guest is Mogol.

What does integral ecology mean?

It is an approach that links environmental sustainability with social, territorial and community themes: not just the environment, but people, culture and territory together.

Is the Festival suitable for a weekend visit to Montefiascone?

Yes: the events are public and designed for occasional visitors too, not only a specialist audience.

What else can I see in Montefiascone alongside the Festival?

Rocca dei Papi, the old town and Lake Bolsena all pair well with a visit that includes the Festival.
