Events and traditions in Montefiascone

The hill town comes alive with historical tales, traditional races, and pageants: discover the famous Wine Festival in August, the rional barrel races, and the historical processions dedicated to the patron saint.

The Wine Festival and Historical Palio Races (August)

The Montefiascone Wine Festival (Fiera del Vino), established in 1950, is the town's most important and eagerly awaited summer event. For fifteen days, historical cellars in the town centre open to host tastings of the local Est! Est!! Est!!! DOC wine, alongside musical performances and cultural events.

The historic streets decorated and crowded during the Wine Festival. Photo courtesy of Fiera del Vino.
Sommeliers and tastings inside the historical wine cellars. Photo courtesy of Fiera del Vino.

The festival features two unique neighborhood competitions: the Palio delle Botti, a spectacular uphill race where strong pushers roll massive wooden barrels through the paved streets of the town, and the Palio della Pigiatura, which pits local districts against each other in a race to crush wine grapes barefoot inside large wooden vats.

The festival culminates with the parade of the Corteo Storico Falisco, a 13th-century style costume procession that re-enacts the arrival of German prelate Johannes Defuk in 1111 and his subsequent funeral pageantry, blending folklore, medieval memory, and theatrical performance.

Patronal Feast of Santa Margherita (July 20th)

The patronal feast honors Saint Margaret of Antioch, the patron saint and protector of Montefiascone, associated with the historical translation of her relics in 1185 by the pilgrim Agostino.

The celebration culminates on July 20th with a solemn religious procession led by the Bishop, flower displays in the historic centre, and major concerts in the squares. The evening ends with a famous fireworks display, lighting up the sky and reflecting on the waters of Lake Bolsena.

ATB Festival - A Tuscia Birra (June/July)

Established in 2011 in the heart of Montefiascone as "A Tuscia Birra", the ATB Festival has grown into one of the most anticipated summer craft beer and indie music festivals in the Lazio region. The event takes place in the characteristic Porticella district, weaving through medieval alleys, historical small squares, and traditional cellars carved into the volcanic tuff.

The lively alleys of the Porticella district during the festival. Photo courtesy of ATB Festival.
Tasting of high-quality craft beers. Photo courtesy of ATB Festival.

Organized by the Libera Voce Association, the festival features a tasting journey showcasing top Italian craft breweries, local street food stands, and multiple stages scattered across the historic center for live music concerts by indie and rock bands, enriched by street artists and traveling performances.

Living Nativity and Solidarity Traditions

During the Christmas season, the Amici del Colle Cultural Association hosts a Living Nativity Scene (Presepe Vivente) in the gardens of the Bishop's Palace, recreating medieval craft shops and historical settings in a unique atmosphere.

Another beloved tradition is the descent of the Befana organized by the AIDO Montefiascone group. Volunteers dressed as the old witch descend by rope from the Clock Tower in the main square to distribute sweets and gifts to children, raising awareness and funds for organ donation.

Planning Your Stay

During the Wine Festival in August, Montefiascone experiences its peak tourist season. We highly recommend booking accommodation and restaurants several weeks in advance if you plan to visit during the weekend of the Palio delle Botti.
