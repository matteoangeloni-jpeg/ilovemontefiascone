Slow Itineraries · Tuscia

Things to see around Montefiascone

A complete 2-3 day itinerary to explore the wonders of Lake Bolsena, the spell of Civita di Bagnoregio, and the mysteries of the Monster Park of Bomarzo.

Itinerary Summary

Duration
2 or 3 days slow
Stops
Bolsena, Marta, Capodimonte, Civita, Bomarzo

Day 1: The Magic of the Lake (Bolsena, Marta, and Capodimonte)

The first day of the itinerary is entirely dedicated to Lake Bolsena, exploring three charming lakefront villages along its eastern and southern shores.

The suggestive sunset over Lake Bolsena. Photo by David Petretti (Scatto Matto).

The first stop is Bolsena, famous for the Rocca Monaldeschi castle (housing the Territorial Museum) and the Basilica of Santa Cristina, home to the 1263 Eucharistic Miracle. In the afternoon, visit Marta, with its 13th-century clock tower, and Capodimonte, dominated by the octagonal Rocca Farnese castle.

The port of Capodimonte provides tourists with the main boarding point for boat trips to Bisentina Island and Martana Island.

Day 2: Suspended Villages and Monstrous Parks (Civita and Bomarzo)

The second day takes visitors inland to discover extraordinary geological landscapes and Renaissance gardens filled with esoteric symbols.

Civita di Bagnoregio stands at 423 meters above sea level and is located about 15 kilometers from Montefiascone (25 minutes by car). The Bomarzo Monster Park (Sacro Bosco) lies at the foot of the Cimini Mountains, commissioned in the 16th century by Prince Vicino Orsini.

The famous 'dying town', perched on a crumbling tuff spur in the Valley of the Calanchi, is accessible only on foot via a scenic pedestrian bridge.

Why Choose Montefiascone as your Travel Base

Staying in Montefiascone represents an optimal logistical and strategic choice for visiting the entire Tuscia region and Lazio's volcanic lakes.

Its elevation of 590 meters ensures cool, breezy summer evenings. Immediate access to the SS2 Cassia highway speeds up travel between Viterbo, Bolsena, Orvieto, and Bagnoregio.

The hilltop village of Montefiascone offers a wider and significantly cheaper range of hotels and B&Bs compared to lakeside towns or iconic spots like Civita di Bagnoregio.

Travel Tips

  • Recommended transport: a car is essential to move around freely.
  • Best seasons: spring (April-June) and early autumn (September-October).
  • Book tickets in advance for Civita di Bagnoregio and the Monster Park.
  • Spend your evenings in Montefiascone dining in historic wine cellars.
