Giglio Vecchio in Montefiascone: Zepponami, Local Sport & Summer Festival

Giglio Vecchio is a historical area on the outskirts of Montefiascone, closely linked to the hamlet of Zepponami. Combining religious heritage, local soccer, and summer music events, it serves as a vibrant community hub.

The Legacy of Madonna del Giglio

In the heart of the Zepponami hamlet stands the Church of the Madonna del Giglio, a historical monument dating back to the 19th century. Built around 1854 to replace an older, smaller chapel in the original Giglio Vecchio district, the church arose from the deep spiritual and community bond between the Capuchin friars and the local residents. Inside, the building features fine decorative paintings completed by the artist Cesare Viali in 1867. The formal inauguration took place on 1 September of that year, while a careful restoration in 1995 returned the structure to its original architectural splendor for the community.

The Amateur Soccer Club ASD Giglio Vecchio

The neighborhood also gives its name to a significant local sporting organization, **ASD Giglio Vecchio**, an amateur sports association based in Montefiascone focused on association football. Regularly registered in the CONI registry since 17 September 2025 and legally represented by Mirko La Corte, the association represents an important social pillar. The establishment of the soccer club and the recent inauguration of its new playing field have provided the Zepponami hamlet with a gathering point for young people and families, highlighting amateur sport as a tool for local inclusion and cohesion.

The Giglio Summer Festival

One of the most anticipated moments of the year is the **Giglio Summer Festival**, held in the hamlet of Zepponami in collaboration with ASD Giglio Vecchio, Genius Music & Events, and MCA Production. In 2026, the event took place from **19 to 21 June**, offering three nights of live music, entertainment, and local food, with street food & drink stalls opening from 7:30 PM. The festival, entirely free of charge, hosted major acts such as Indie Power, MCA Showcase, and Non C'è Band starting at 10:00 PM (preceded by warm-ups at 9:00 PM). The second edition concluded with great success, drawing a large crowd and earning warm thanks from the organizers to the staff, local youths, and sponsors.

Why Visit Giglio Vecchio

Exploring Giglio Vecchio and Zepponami lets you connect with the authentic, everyday life of Montefiascone. Far from being just a passing locality, this area showcases 19th-century devotion, a strong community sport ethos, and a flair for summer musical festivals that attract visitors from across the Tuscia region. It is the perfect stop for travelers looking to discover local life beyond the main tourist routes.

What to Combine with Your Visit

Given its proximity to the main hub of Montefiascone, a visit to Zepponami pairs perfectly with a walk through the town's historic center to view the majestic dome of Santa Margherita or a scenic stop at the Rocca dei Papi. From here, it is very easy to head down to the shores of **Lake Bolsena** for a sunset or plan a tasting itinerary along the Est! Est!! Est!!! wine route.

Before You Go

Zepponami is easily accessible by car or via local public transport from Montefiascone. The sports field and the church are the area's main landmarks. For temporary events like the summer festival, it is always recommended to verify event timings and parking options in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Giglio Vecchio located?

Giglio Vecchio is a historical area located in the hamlet (frazione) of Zepponami, a few kilometers south of Montefiascone's center.

What is the historical significance of the Madonna del Giglio Church?

Built in 1854 and inaugurated in 1867, it houses beautiful murals by Cesare Viali and represents the historic connection between local residents and Capuchin friars.

What is ASD Giglio Vecchio?

It is a local amateur soccer club, registered with CONI in 2025, which manages sporting and recreational activities at the Zepponami sports field.
