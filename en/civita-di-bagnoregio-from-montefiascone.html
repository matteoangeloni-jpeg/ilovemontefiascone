Civita di Bagnoregio from Montefiascone

Civita di Bagnoregio, the famous 'dying town' perched on a tuff outcrop, is one of Tuscia's most striking day trips. From Montefiascone it pairs easily with a Lake Bolsena itinerary. Here is what to consider before you go and how to plan the visit.

At a glance

What it is
The 'dying town'
Height
About 423 m
Access
On foot, pedestrian bridge

Why pair it with Montefiascone

Civita di Bagnoregio lies about 15 km from Montefiascone (roughly 25 minutes by car, to be checked for traffic and season), which makes it a natural day trip for anyone staying near Lake Bolsena. Pairing the 'dying town' with Montefiascone links two of Tuscia's signature landscapes within a short drive.

What to know before you go

Civita sits at about 423 m on a tuff outcrop in the Valle dei Calanchi and is reached only on foot via a pedestrian bridge. Entry may require a ticket and involves a climb: check tickets, hours and accessibility with official sources before travelling, as they can change.

The visit and nearby

The visit centres on the small hilltop village and its views over the valley, best in the morning or late afternoon for the light. Allow for the bridge walk and crowds in peak periods. Combine it with the Lake Bolsena villages; to reach the area, see how to get to Montefiascone and consider a weekend.

Frequently asked questions

How far is Civita di Bagnoregio from Montefiascone?

About 15 km, roughly 25 minutes by car. Actual times depend on traffic and season, so check the route with a navigation app.

How do you enter Civita di Bagnoregio?

On foot, via a pedestrian bridge. Entry may require a ticket: check prices, hours and accessibility with official sources before going.

Can you visit it on a day trip from Montefiascone?

Yes, it is an ideal day trip, easy to combine with Lake Bolsena. The hilltop village is compact but involves a walk uphill across the bridge.
