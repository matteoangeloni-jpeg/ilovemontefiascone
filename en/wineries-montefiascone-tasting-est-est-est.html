Wineries in Montefiascone: tasting guide

Discover wineries in Montefiascone, tastings, Est! Est!! Est!!! DOC wine, volcanic terroir, and food pairings between Lake Bolsena and Tuscia.

Key Grapes

Volcanic Terroir and DOC Specifications

The Est! Est!! Est!!! of Montefiascone DOC is born in a volcanic area gravitating around the slopes of the Volsini complex, overlooking the basin of Lake Bolsena.

Typical grapes and vines of Est! Est!! Est!!! celebrated during the historic Wine Fair. Photo by Wine Fair.

The official blend of the DOC requires Trebbiano Toscano (Procanico) between 50% and 65%, Rossetto (Trebbiano Giallo) between 25% and 40%, and Malvasia Bianca Lunga and/or del Lazio between 10% and 20%.

The volcanic tuffaceous and sandy soils of the Volsini area, rich in potassium and mineral salts, add a distinct mineral edge and savory finish to the local white wine.

Antica Cantina Leonardi

Antica Cantina Leonardi is a historic winery founded in 1910 by Domenico Leonardi, famous for its monumental cellars carved by hand into the volcanic tuff of Montefiascone.

The tradition of historic wine cellars carved into the volcanic tuff. Photo by Wine Fair.

The winery offers structured tasting itineraries (such as "The Family Wines") pairing local wines with regional cold cuts and cheeses from the Tuscia area.

The estate focuses on the valorization of native grape varieties such as Roscetto, vinified both in traditional blends and as monovarietal labels in pureness.

Cantina Stefanoni

Cantina Stefanoni is a family-run farm started in the early 1950s by Genuino Stefanoni and located in via Bandita in Montefiascone.

The estate collaborates with the University of Perugia, managing an experimental vineyard for the study, clonal selection, and promotion of the native Roscetto grape.

Stefanoni's range includes classic method sparkling wines from Roscetto grapes, dry whites, structured reds, and the traditional sweet Aleatico of Tuscia.

Cantina di Montefiascone

The Cantina di Montefiascone is a large cooperative winery founded in 1956 that groups hundreds of local grape growers, managing a large share of the DOC's production.

The cooperative offers the "Degusta" reception format, featuring guided tasting and food pairing paths dedicated to promoting Est! Est!! Est!!! and organic labels.

The cooperative bottles and markets a wide selection of labels under the Est! Est!! Est!!! and Tuscia DOC appellations, ensuring full traceability of its members' grapes.

What to Eat in Montefiascone: Food Pairings

The gastronomy of Montefiascone and Lake Bolsena blends the delicate flavors of lake fish with the rustic pasta dishes of Faliscan peasant tradition.

Typical dishes include Lake Coregone (grilled or in green sauce), Lombrichelli of Tuscia (handmade water-and-flour pasta), and the savory Bolsena Anguilla (eel, often stewed).

The fresh, citrusy, and mineral profile of Est! Est!! Est!!! DOC pairs ideally with the delicate texture of Coregone, cleansing the palate with its crisp acidity.
