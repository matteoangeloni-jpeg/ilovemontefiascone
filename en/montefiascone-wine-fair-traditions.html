Why Attend the Montefiascone Wine Fair

The Wine Fair is the signature event of the summer in Montefiascone. Perched on the edge of the ancient volcanic caldera overlooking Lake Bolsena, the town offers the perfect backdrop to sample outstanding local wines. During warm August evenings, the historic center turns into a lively open-air market. Historic cellars open their doors, and local winemakers guide visitors through tastings of the region’s traditional flavors.

The Legend of Est! Est!! Est!!! and Giovanni Defuk

Montefiascone’s identity is bound to its famous white DOC wine, **Est! Est!! Est!!!**. The fair celebrates a tradition dating back to 1111 AD. According to legend, a German bishop named Giovanni Defuk sent his servant Martino ahead to mark the inns serving the best wines by writing the word "Est" (Latin for "It is") on their doors. Martino was so impressed by Montefiascone's wine that he wrote "Est! Est!! Est!!!" in excitement. Defuk agreed and enjoyed the wine so much that he stayed in the town until his death. Today, the fair commemorates this story with a spectacular **historic parade in medieval dress**, featuring drums, flag-throwers, and noble characters filling the historic streets.

What to Combine with Your Visit

Visiting during the Wine Fair is an excellent opportunity to experience the town's history and scenery. By day, you can explore the **Rocca dei Papi** (the Popes' Fortress) for an unmatched view of Lake Bolsena, or visit the two-story **Basilica of San Flaviano**, where Bishop Defuk is buried under a legendary epitaph. As dusk falls, join the tasting paths in the old town, pairing a chilled glass of Est! Est!! Est!!! with typical local dishes like lake whitefish (coregone) or artisanal cold cuts.

Dates and Practical Planning

In 2026, the 66th Wine Fair takes place on **1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, and 14 August**. Festivities begin around 7:00 PM. The historic core is fully pedestrianized during the event. It is recommended to leave vehicles in parking lots outside the town walls (such as Campo Boario) and walk in. The day-by-day event schedule is typically published close to the start date on official municipal channels.
