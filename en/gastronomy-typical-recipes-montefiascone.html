Gastronomy and typical recipes of Montefiascone

From the shores of the lake to the volcanic land of the Volsini hills: the typical cuisine of Montefiascone blends the delicate flavor of freshwater fish with the rustic richness of Tuscia's peasant soups.

La Sbroscia: Lake Bolsena fish soup

La Sbroscia is the most characteristic lake fish soup of Montefiascone and the wider lake basin. Originally conceived as a one-pot meal prepared by fishermen directly on their boats or on the shores of the lake, it uses the fresh catch of the day.

The traditional recipe features tench, pike, coregone, perch, and eel cooked in a fragrant broth with fresh tomatoes, onion, garlic, thickly cut potatoes, and local wild mint (mentuccia). It is served steaming hot over slices of stale country bread, lightly rubbed with garlic and toasted.

The crisp, mineral, and citrusy character of Est! Est!! Est!!! DOC Classico cuts beautifully through the rich oils of the eel and lake fish, leaving the palate clean and refreshed.

L'Acquacotta of Tuscia

L'Acquacotta represents the quintessence of peasant cooking in the Viterbo area. Once a humble meal consumed by shepherds and cowboys (butteri) during long days in the fields, it relies on wild chicory and simple pantry items.

The base consists of wild chicory boiled with potatoes, garlic, tomato, and wild mint. The soup is enriched in the final minutes of cooking with a poached egg ("uovo sperso") or salted cod, poured boiling hot over slices of stale bread, and finished with raw extra virgin olive oil.

La Scafata and typical desserts: Tozzetti of Tuscia

Scafata is a typical spring soup made with fresh baby broad beans (called scafi in dialect), tender artichokes, spring onions, chard, and crispy pork cheek (guanciale), gently simmered to highlight the garden's fresh flavors.

For dessert, Tozzetti of Tuscia are the undisputed stars. Similar to Tuscan cantucci in their elongated shape and double-baked crunch, they differ by using local Cimini hazelnuts (Gentile Romana) instead of almonds. They are traditionally dunked in Cannaiola di Marta DOC, a sweet, fresh, and aromatic red wine from the southern shore of the lake.

Festivals and Food Events

To taste these recipes in their most authentic setting, do not miss the Sagra della Sbroscia held on the shores of the lake in summer, and the food stands set up during the Wine Fair in August.
