Things to see in Montefiascone: complete guide

An exhaustive overview to explore the village of the Popes between the medieval fortress, the grand dome of Santa Margherita, the historic basilica of San Flaviano, and local highlights.

Guide Summary

Key Themes
Papal history, Via Francigena, Est! Est!! Est!!!
Key Locations
Rocca dei Papi, Santa Margherita, San Flaviano

History and Charm of the Popes' Village

Montefiascone presents itself as a terrace of stone and domes suspended at 590 meters above sea level on the highest peak of the Volsini Mountains, offering exceptional strategic control over the basin of Lake Bolsena.

In the Middle Ages, Pope Innocent III chose Montefiascone as the seat of the Rector of the Patrimony of St. Peter in Tuscia. Later, in the 14th century, Cardinal Egidio Albornoz used the fortress as an operational base to re-establish papal authority in the region.

The strategic proximity to Rome made Montefiascone the perfect and secure refuge for the popes during periods of strong political instability in the capital of the Papal States.

The Rocca dei Papi and the Pilgrim's Tower

The Rocca dei Papi is the monumental site situated at the highest point of the village of Montefiascone, originally conceived as a military stronghold and residential palace for the popes.

The Rocca dei Papi dominates from the top of the hill, offering a complete panorama over Lake Bolsena.

The structure houses halls dedicated to Innocent III and Leo X. Work on the fortress was completed in 1516 under the direction of the famous architect Antonio da Sangallo the Younger.

The Pilgrim's Tower, soaring within the Rocca dei Papi, offers visitors a complete 360-degree panoramic view spanning the entire Lake Bolsena and the hills of Tuscia.

The Cathedral of Santa Margherita and its Imposing Dome

The Cathedral of Santa Margherita is the main church of Montefiascone, dedicated to the local patron saint, and is characterized by a spacious octagonal floor plan.

The majestic painted dome seen from inside the Cathedral. Photo by David Petretti (Scatto Matto).

The spectacular dome of the cathedral measures 27 meters in diameter and was completed in 1674 by the architect Carlo Fontana following a devastating fire in 1670.

The crypt beneath the Cathedral of Santa Margherita houses the historic remains of Saint Lucia Filippini and preserves the memory of Cardinal Marco Antonio Barbarigo.

The Basilica of San Flaviano: Unique Architecture and Mysteries

The Basilica of San Flaviano is a singular Romanesque-Gothic temple located along the Via Cassia, consisting of two superimposed churches characterized by opposing liturgical orientations.

The historical landscape surrounding the Basilica of San Flaviano and the hills of Montefiascone.

The lower church dates back to the 11th century and features a dense cycle of medieval frescoes painted between the 14th and 16th centuries, notably the stories of St. Catherine of Alexandria.

The left aisle of the Basilica of San Flaviano preserves the historic gravestone of Giovanni Defuk, the German prelate at the heart of the famous legend of Est! Est!! Est!!!.

The Legend of Est! Est!! Est!!! and the Tomb of Giovanni Defuk

The legend of the Est! Est!! Est!!! of Montefiascone DOC wine dates back to the year 1111, during the trip to Rome of the German prelate Johannes Defuk (Giovanni Defuk) in the entourage of Emperor Henry V.

Guided tasting of Est! Est!! Est!!! DOC in the historic underground wine cellars. Photo by Wine Fair.

The servant Martino preceded the prelate, marking the doors of inns with excellent wine with the word "Est" (it is here). In Montefiascone, Martino wrote the signal three times, creating the wine's famous name.

Giovanni Defuk, captivated by the quality of the local wine, prolonged his stay in Montefiascone until his death in 1113, and was buried inside the basilica of San Flaviano.

Active Tourism: The Via Francigena and Lake Bolsena

Montefiascone is a crucial hub of the Via Francigena in northern Lazio, positioning itself as the starting point of Stage 40, which leads pilgrims towards Viterbo.

The Montefiascone-Viterbo stage covers 17.8 kilometers. The first segment of the walk offers a spectacular passage over the authentic Roman paving stones of the ancient Via Cassia.

The hilltop plateau of the village offers a convenient logistical base for combining historic walks with water sports (kayaking, sailing) on the shores of nearby Lake Bolsena.

Events Not to Miss: The Wine Festival and Historical Re-enactments

Cultural life in Montefiascone peaks in the summer months, with historical, artistic, and gastronomic events of regional importance.

The Montefiascone Wine Fair, which reached its 65th edition in 2025, takes place in August and celebrates the harvest and the legend of Defuk. The Est Film Festival has animated late July evenings since 2007.

The Historical Faliscan Procession marches during the Wine Fair to re-enact in period costumes the legendary events of the servant Martino and Giovanni Defuk.
