# Content Backlog - Organic Growth

Updated: 2026-07-02  
Branch: `main`

## Goal

Move the site from a technically clean multilingual perimeter to a more discoverable and better interconnected organic asset without creating new public HTML pages in this phase.

## Backlog Structure

These are the highest-value refinements because they affect crawl paths, user orientation and cluster clarity.

1. Strengthen homepage pathways to the main hubs:
   - `cosa-vedere`
   - `eventi`
   - `lago-di-bolsena`
   - `vino`
   - `via-francigena-bolsena-montefiascone`

   - single event spokes

3. Keep the Lake Bolsena system semantically split:

4. Keep the wine system semantically split:

   - Est! Est!! Est!!! identity page

### P1 - Cluster Strengthening

These tasks improve organic growth without changing the perimeter.

1. Add or refine internal links between:
   - home and core hubs
   - `cosa-vedere` and monument spokes
   - `eventi` and individual event pages
   - `lago-di-bolsena` and village / itinerary spokes
   - `vino` and tasting / DOC / cantina spokes
   - `via-francigena` and packing / no-car / trekking spokes

2. Improve EN wording where pages are still too close to Italian structure.

3. Improve DE topical depth on the weakest support pages while keeping the same URLs and layout.

4. Reduce role overlap between:
   - `guide`
   - `cosa-vedere`
   - `cosa-vedere-montefiascone-guida-completa`
   - `montefiascone-travel-guide`
   - `reisefuehrer-montefiascone`

5. Make monument pages easier to discover from the discovery hub.

### P2 - Support and Long-Tail Growth

These are valuable but lower urgency.

1. Expand photo and visual discovery links:
   - gallery

2. Strengthen food and stay pathways:
   - what to eat
   - where to eat
   - where to sleep
   - local food and recipes

3. Reinforce civic / trust pages:
   - associations and volunteering
   - tourist information

4. Add more explicit cross-links from utility pages back to the hubs.

## Editorial Rule for Growth

Any future content improvement should answer one of these user needs:

- what to see
- what to do
- how to move
- where to go next
- what to taste
- when to go
- why Montefiascone should be the base

If a proposed addition does not map to one of those needs, it should stay out of the backlog.
